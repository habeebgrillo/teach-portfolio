Pedagogy Portfolio

My name is Habeeb Grillo. I am a computing lecturer and software engineer with experience teaching across further education programmes, including BTEC and T Level provision. This portfolio presents my pedagogical approach, underpinned by educational theory, inclusive practice, and industry-relevant teaching methods.

The portfolio evidences how theory informs my planning, teaching, assessment, and learner support, with a particular focus on preparing learners for progression into further study and employment within the digital and technology sectors.

Key Pedagogical Influences

Lev Vygotsky

Vygotsky’s social constructivist theory informs my use of scaffolding, collaborative learning, and guided practice, particularly within problem-solving and coding activities.

David Kolb

Kolb’s experiential learning cycle aligns closely with project-based learning in computing, where learners design, build, reflect, and refine digital solutions.

John Sweller

Cognitive Load Theory informs my lesson planning by ensuring complex technical concepts are sequenced and presented in manageable stages.

Abraham Maslow

Maslow’s hierarchy of needs underpins my commitment to creating safe, supportive learning environments where learners feel confident to engage and take risks.

Carol Ann Tomlinson

Tomlinson’s work on differentiation informs my approach to inclusive teaching, ensuring tasks are accessible while maintaining high expectations.

Ralph Tyler

Tyler’s curriculum rationale supports structured curriculum design aligned to clear learning objectives, assessment, and progression routes.

Using This Portfolio

The portfolio is structured around learning outcomes 1 to 6, each exploring a different aspect of professional teaching practice, including learner support, inclusive practice, assessment, safeguarding, and curriculum design.

Each section combines educational theory with reflective examples from my teaching practice, demonstrating how research-informed pedagogy supports learner engagement, achievement, and progression within computing education.
