3.1 - Explain how learners’ background and experiences can affect their needs, aspirations, wider expectations, decision-making abilities, and approach to learning

Learners’ backgrounds and life experiences significantly influence their educational needs, aspirations, expectations, and approaches to learning. Factors such as socioeconomic background, prior educational experiences, cultural norms, and family expectations can shape learners’ confidence, motivation, and engagement (Bourdieu, 1986).

In computing education, learners from digitally privileged backgrounds may appear confident but lack depth of understanding, while learners with limited access to technology may possess strong problem-solving skills but lower self-belief. These differences can influence learners’ willingness to participate, ask questions, or take risks.

By recognising these influences, I can adapt teaching to be culturally responsive and inclusive. Avoiding assumptions about prior knowledge, providing explicit explanations, and using varied examples allow learners to engage meaningfully and make informed decisions about their learning and progression pathways.


3.2 - Analyse the impact of different cognitive, physical, and sensory abilities on learning

Differences in cognitive processing, physical ability, and sensory perception can significantly affect how learners engage with learning activities. In computing, where abstract thinking, logic, and sustained concentration are required, learners may experience cognitive overload if information is poorly structured (Sweller, 1988).

To address this, I break complex concepts into manageable stages, use visual representations such as diagrams and flowcharts, and provide opportunities for guided practice. This supports learners in building understanding progressively rather than becoming overwhelmed.

Reasonable adjustments, including assistive technologies, alternative formats, and flexible delivery methods, ensure learners with physical or sensory needs can access learning and demonstrate achievement equitably. These adaptations support inclusion without lowering academic standards.


3.3 - Describe barriers to learning and ways to overcome them, including adapting teaching, learning, and assessment

Barriers to learning may include language difficulties, anxiety, disability, limited access to technology, or external pressures such as employment or caring responsibilities. If unaddressed, these barriers can negatively impact attendance, engagement, and achievement.

Inclusive teaching strategies include adapting delivery, learning activities, and assessment methods. For example, learners may be allowed to submit screen-recorded explanations of code or practical demonstrations as alternative assessment methods, rather than relying solely on extended written responses. Flexible deadlines and additional support can also help learners manage external pressures while maintaining progress.

Such adaptations ensure learning remains accessible and fair, supporting learners to overcome barriers and achieve positive outcomes.

To support learner understanding of abstract online world concepts, I produced a short instructional video for Level 2 ICT learners. The resource combines narrated explanation with visual analogies to support learners who benefit from auditory and visual learning approaches.

Captions were provided to support accessibility and inclusive learning, summarising key concepts discussed within the video to support understanding for learners who rely on text-based reinforcement.

This resource enabled learners to revisit key content independently, supporting consolidation outside of taught sessions. Learner feedback indicated that the use of analogies improved understanding of complex concepts such as online services and data flow. The video also supported inclusive practice by allowing learners to control pace and revisit explanations as needed.

This approach also supports learner independence by enabling revision at an individual pace, which is particularly beneficial for learners who may require additional time to process information.
